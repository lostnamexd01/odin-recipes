Neapolitan Pizza

Back to main page

Description

Neapolitan pizza is a traditional Italian pizza that originated in Naples. It is characterized by its thin, soft crust, simple toppings, and high-quality ingredients. The classic version, Pizza Margherita, features tomatoes, fresh mozzarella cheese, fresh basil, and a drizzle of olive oil.

Ingredients

  • 500g (4 cups) all-purpose flour
  • 325ml (1⅓ cups) water
  • 10g (2 tsp) salt
  • 2g (½ tsp) active dry yeast
  • 200g (7 oz) San Marzano tomatoes, crushed
  • 250g (9 oz) fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt. In a separate bowl, dissolve the yeast in water.
  2. Gradually add the water to the flour mixture, stirring until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise for 2 hours at room temperature.
  5. Preheat your oven to its highest setting (usually around 250°C or 482°F).
  6. Divide the dough into 2-3 equal portions and shape each into a ball. Let them rest for another 30 minutes.
  7. On a floured surface, stretch each dough ball into a thin circle.
  8. Spread crushed San Marzano tomatoes over the base, leaving a small border around the edges.
  9. Add slices of fresh mozzarella and a few basil leaves on top.
  10. Drizzle with extra virgin olive oil and bake in the preheated oven for about 7-10 minutes until the crust is golden and crispy.
  11. Remove from the oven, add more fresh basil if desired, slice, and serve hot.
